Harmful, yet ‘normal’ stress into everyday life:



As society developed into what it is today, more and more expectation and stress is pushed onto teens and young adults. For example, consider how much stress is placed on high school students: increasingly-difficult courses, projects, extracurricular activities, part-time jobs, at-home responsibilities, social environments, college-applications, college and career preparation, and anything else that may be applicable. Society throws everything onto these still-developing adolescents, expecting them to not end up being increasingly-stressed as those four years go by.

If you have been trying to work hard, be productive, or ‘lock in’ and suddenly you just couldn’t anymore–it’s not your fault. It’s the way this system was designed to maximize success but also, accidentally, stress. For you to understand this is the most important step towards prevention and recovery.
