Ever heard of ‘burnout?’ It’s a lot more real than you might think.

If you don’t know what it is, burnout is a prolonged feeling of exhaustion and withdrawal from what once was a period of intense productivity and success. While burnout is not an official condition, it is an unrecognized form of depression. Depression, especially in the form of burnout, is directly caused by one particular social norm: chronic stress.

  • Many teens and adults experience this feeling of being burnt-out either in high school or college.
  • Productivity has become the expectation for teens and young adults in education, but so much so to the point that the line blurs between ‘productivity’ and ‘chronic stress.’
  • While it is important to be productive with education and jobs, doing so in an unhealthy manner will only backfire and lead to burnout.
  • It is possible to have a good high-school or college life and turn out successful, but it requires knowing how to recognize and alleviate intense and/or chronic sources of stress.
